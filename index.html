Margaret Weis & Tracy Hickman

Weis and Hickman are some of the early pioneers of DnD. They worked for TSR, who became Wizards of the Coast, and created the first Fantasy and Sci-Fi series I ever read - DragonLance - and the rest was history. There are currently over 150 books and collections of tales set in the DragonLance universe.

Robert Jordan

Following DragonLance, I found Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time series, which is now a major screwup television series produced by Amazon. Started in the '80s, this epic high fantasy series was heavily influenced by multiple fantasy books - mainly Tolkein's Lord of the Rings trilogy. WoT is a 14 book series (the first 11 written by Robert Jordan; see Brandon Sanderson) totalling over 4.4M words spanning over 10,000 hardback book pages. I've read all of these books at least 3 times, and many of them upwards of 7 times. Stop looking at me like that!

Brandon Sanderson

I first heard of Brandon Sanderson when he was tapped to take over for Robert Jordan upon Jordan's death in late 2007. While I waited 6 more years for the conclusion of the WoT tale, I began to read some of Sanderson and fell in love with his works. The Mistborn series is an incredible read, with a very novel and unique magic system. Sanderson is current working on an epic high fantasy series called The Stormlight Archive, another behemoth group of novels currently sitting at just under 1.8M words spanning over 5,000 hardback pages.

Roger Zelazny

Started in the '70s, the Chronicles of Amber is a set of 10 books written by Zelazny until his death in 1991. Tragically, Zelazny had planned for more novels while simultaneously forbidding anyone else to finish the series. Even knowing his death was imminent, Zelazny did nothing to permit others to finish his series. The fact that someone tried (and failed, if you read the reviews!) is hardly relevant, since no one could possibly embue Amber books with the same vitality and personality.

Stephen King

I'm not a fan of horror, but his writing style is undeniable. I've read most of his books, including the horor, but his fantasy fiction is my real draw. The Dark Tower series (made into a crappy movie, like many, many SK novels and short stories) is a series of 7 books and starts with the first book King ever wrote, way back in college, although it was not his first book published. It's an amazong tale modeled after wild west stories and brought to life by the Browning poem Childe Rowland to the Dark Tower Came. A near-death accident struck King after the first 3 books and nearly ended the tale prematurely, however King continued on to write the final 4 books. (Spoiler:

King wrote his accident into the remaining novels as a key plot point, forever changing anything he could have planned as the remainder of the novel. This fact almost made me loathe the series, but time and successive re-reads softened my view, until I eventually came to love the series as written.
